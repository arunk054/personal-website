Thanks for visiting my webpage.
I am third year PhD student in the Institute for Software Research (ISR) at Carnegie Mellon University, where I am advised by Prof. Jim Herbsleb.
My research is about studying collaboration in online communities such as open-source software and Wikipedia. I use a broad spectrum of techniques in my research that can be categorized into the following three streams.
  • Qualitative analysis through interviews / surveys, and content analysis of archival data.
  • Statistical and machine learning techniques to build models that are often informed by findings from my qualitative analysis. These models are used to predict various outcomes of a collaboration and are primarily built for datasets mined from Github, Wikipedia and mailing lists.
  • Simulations often developed from agent-based models to perform virtual experiments and occasionally use network analysis to find key entities and study the evolution of ties in a community.
Broadly, I am interested in social media analytics, applied machine learning, multi-agent systems and distributed computing.
