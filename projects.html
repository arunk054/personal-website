Sharing and Co-Creation in Scientific Software
The project aims at studying the process of software development and sharing in collaborative science. I work with a post doc and a graduate student. We are exploring various themes such as the role of community code engagements, cost of coordination and code maintenance.
Effects of concurrent modifications on quality of articles in Wikipedia
The proximity of edits in time and space(e.g. same paragraph, line, etc.) can be measured to understand the level of concurrent modifications to an article. We computed these concurrency measures on a subset of Wikipedia articles - randomly chosen 'good' and 'featured' class articles. A comparative study was performed to understand the difference between the two classes of articles.[Report]
An Agent Based Model of Edit Warring on Wikipedia
Edit warring happens when editors disagree on a contentious issue in an article. These edit wars may or may not result in consensus. Predicting whether an on going edit war will lead to consensus is challenging. In this project we propose a game theoretic model of the process of Edit War to predict various outcomes of a given collaboration of editors such as the time taken to reach consensus, efficiency and level of social influence among the editors. [Paper]
